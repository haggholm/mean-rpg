Dice in detail

To recapitulate, when performing a check with the dice, you

  1. Roll ; that is, roll a ten-sided die, and if you roll a 10, roll again and add up (as long as you keep rolling tens).
  2. Add up any bonuses.
  3. Subtract the difficulty.
    • If you are competing against a living opponent, the “difficulty” is represented by their doing a roll just like yours, i.e. + bonuses.
  4. See if you succeed (if the result is over 0), fail (if it’s under 0), or “stalemate” (if it’s 0).
  5. Keep track of the difference value (i.e. how close to 0 you were).

I hope we can agree that’s fairly straightforward, but I’m sure we can agree that it’s not obvious what this actually means in terms of outcome. If your bonuses add up to +2 above your opponent, whats is the chance that you’ll win the roll? The answer is About 55%, but it’s not quite intutive, what with potentially-unlimited rerolls and so forth.
